The problem02
01.
Variable ratio reinforcement
Unpredictable rewards (the post that makes you laugh, the video you didn't expect) are the most powerful reinforcement schedule known. Slot machines use the same mechanism — and so does your feed.
02.
The window of tolerance
Dan Siegel's model shows that intentional choice is only available inside a specific arousal range. Above it, behaviour becomes automatic. A 90-second reset brings you back into the window.
03.
Attention residue
Unfinished states leave a cognitive trace. Without a clean boundary, your attention is split across everything you half-finished scrolling through. The reset closes those open loops.

The Neural Reset Session03
I
Interrupt
5 – 10 s
Screen dims. The orb slows. One line of text. No stats, no judgment — just a pause that breaks the loop's momentum.
"Pause. You went somewhere. You're back now."
II
Discharge
30 – 45 s
Orb-paced breathing: 4s inhale, 2s hold, 6s exhale. The longer exhale activates the vagus nerve. This is physiology, not aesthetic.
"Let the orb breathe for you."
III
Re-anchor
30 – 60 s
Four choices. Equal weight. No recommendation. Continue intentionally · Switch task · Take a break · Close this site. The choice is yours — and now it actually is.
"What's actually next?"
IV
Reinforce
5 s
The orb settles. One sentence closes the loop — not a reward, not a streak. A reframe that builds identity over time.
"That was recovery. You're building something."

Attention residue
Unfinished tasks leave a cognitive trace that degrades the next thing you touch. The interrupt phase creates a boundary — ending the previous state before the next begins.
Sophie Leroy · Organizational Behavior and Human Decision Processes, 109(2), 168–181 · UW Bothell, 2009
Interruption recovery
Interrupted workers take an average of 23 minutes and 15 seconds to resume focused work — passing through two intervening tasks first. The reset creates a natural boundary, making re-entry lower-cost.
Gloria Mark · "The Cost of Interrupted Work: More Speed and Stress" · CHI 2008 · UC Irvine
Exhale-dominant breathing
Slow breathing with longer exhales measurably increases parasympathetic tone — confirmed across multiple studies using heart rate variability as the index. The 4-in / 6-out pattern used in the reset sits within the well-studied 0.1 Hz resonance range.
Laborde et al. · Neuroscience & Biobehavioral Reviews, 2022 · peer-reviewed meta-analysis
Identity-based habit
Framing behaviour as skill-building rather than performance increases long-term repetition. The goal is not app engagement — it is making the app unnecessary.
Wendy Wood · Good Habits, Bad Habits · USC, 2019 · popularised by James Clear, Atomic Habits, 2018
